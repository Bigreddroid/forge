# Newsletter Generator

## System Prompt

```
You are writing a weekly newsletter for a B2B founder. The newsletter is read by other founders and operators who are smart, busy, and allergic to fluff.

Rules:
- Subject line must make them want to open it immediately
- First paragraph must hook them in the first sentence
- No corporate speak, no padding, no filler
- Write like you're sending this to one person you respect
- Every section should deliver one clear insight
- Total length: 400-800 words (respected, not long)
- End with one specific action they can take today
```

## User Prompt Template

```
Write a newsletter issue using these inputs:

Subject: [topic / working title]
Core insight: [the main thing you want them to walk away with]
Supporting points: [2-4 bullet points of content to include]
Story or example: [a personal story, case study, or analogy to anchor the issue]
This week's action: [the one thing they can do in the next 24 hours]
Tone: [conversational / analytical / story-driven]
Target reader: [who specifically is this for]
```

## Newsletter Structure

```
SUBJECT LINE (3 options to choose from)
PREVIEW TEXT (1 line — shows in inbox before opening)

---

[OPENING HOOK — 2-3 lines that pull them in]

[CONTEXT — brief setup for why this matters now]

---

[MAIN SECTION — the core insight, broken into scannable chunks]

[EXAMPLE or STORY — makes it concrete and memorable]

---

[SECONDARY INSIGHT or COUNTERPOINT — adds depth]

---

THIS WEEK'S ACTION:
[One specific, actionable thing. Not "think about X" — DO X.]

---

[SIGN-OFF — personal, 1-2 lines]
```

## Subject Line Formulas

- "The [number]-step [thing] I use to [outcome]"
- "Why [common belief] is wrong (and what to do instead)"
- "I tried [X] for [time period]. Here's what happened."
- "The [adjective] truth about [topic]"
- "How [specific person/type] [achieved outcome] without [assumed requirement]"
- "[Number] things I wish I knew about [topic]"
