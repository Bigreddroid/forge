# Market & Competitor Analysis Prompt

## Competitor Content Monitor

```
You are a market intelligence analyst. Review the following content from [COMPETITOR NAME] and provide:

1. POSITIONING SUMMARY (2-3 sentences): What narrative are they building? Who are they speaking to?

2. CONTENT GAPS: What topics are they NOT covering that their audience probably cares about?

3. ANGLE OPPORTUNITIES: What counterarguments or alternative takes could establish a stronger position?

4. MESSAGING WEAKNESSES: What claims feel weak, vague, or unsubstantiated?

5. ONE ACTION: What's the single most valuable thing to publish in response to this?

Content to analyze:
[PASTE COMPETITOR CONTENT]
```

## ICP Research Prompt

```
You are a customer research analyst. Based on the following data sources, build a detailed Ideal Customer Profile:

Sources provided:
- [Sales call transcripts / interview notes / survey responses / LinkedIn comments]

Output format:

DEMOGRAPHICS:
- Title(s) most likely to buy
- Company size and stage
- Industry
- Geography

PSYCHOGRAPHICS:
- What they believe about [your category]
- What they've tried before
- Why previous solutions failed them

LANGUAGE THEY USE:
- Exact phrases they use to describe their problem
- Words they use for the outcome they want
- Objections in their own words

BUYING TRIGGERS:
- Events that make them start looking for a solution
- What they search for when they're ready

BIGGEST INSIGHT:
- The one non-obvious thing about this buyer that most competitors miss

Data to analyze:
[PASTE DATA]
```

## Trend Identification Prompt

```
You are scanning the following content from the last 7 days in [INDUSTRY/NICHE]. Identify:

1. RISING TOPICS: What themes are appearing more frequently?
2. SHIFTING SENTIMENT: What previously accepted beliefs are being questioned?
3. EMERGING VOCABULARY: What new terms or frameworks are appearing?
4. CONTENT OPPORTUNITIES: What questions are being asked that nobody is answering well?
5. THIS WEEK'S ANGLE: One specific post idea that would be timely and differentiated right now.

Content sources:
[PASTE AGGREGATED CONTENT HEADLINES/SUMMARIES]
```
